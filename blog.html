UNSW Heroes Program

The UNSW Heroes Program was a great experience both for personal and professional development.
There were multiple workshops for students to extend their knowledge. This would be useful later
to develop ideas which could improve the university services. We were placed in teams and tasked
with designing service improvements for the university.

Through this program, I developed my leadership and communication skills. I was actually chosen by
my team to pitch at the Service Improvement Conference for Semester 2 of 2017. In the end, my team
came 3rd out of 13 teams which was a remarkable achievement.

EWB Design Summit

The EWB Design Summit was a student study tour focused on the role of Human Centred Design in an
humanitarian engineering context. This was a great opportunity to develop my professional skills for
engineering as well as communication and problem solving skills. We were faced by many challenges
throughout the trip but overcame them through perseverance and teamwork.

Droid Racing Challenge (DRC)

The Droid Racing Challenge was a project aimed at using computer vision to automate an RC car.
This involved using C++ and OpenCV on a Raspberry Pi to create a self-driving/autonomous vehicle.
This was done in teams of 6, and my team (Team Pokemon) came in the top 4 teams.

Unfortunately on the final day of testing, our car failed in the last hour of testing. However we
still did quite well and I learnt a lot about computer vision and how it can be used for
automating vehicles.
